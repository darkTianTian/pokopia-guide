# All Crafting Materials in Pokémon Pokopia

This is a comprehensive guide to every crafting material in Pokémon Pokopia. Whether you need Lumber for building, Brick for construction, or Paper for decorations, this guide covers where to find raw materials and which Pokémon specialties you need to process them.

## How Materials Work

Materials in Pokopia come in two forms:

- **Raw materials** — picked up from the ground, obtained by breaking objects, or dropped by Pokémon with the ![icon:Litter](/images/specialties/litter.png) Litter specialty
- **Processed materials** — created by giving raw materials to Pokémon with specific specialties (![icon:Chop](/images/specialties/chop.png) Chop, ![icon:Burn](/images/specialties/burn.png) Burn, ![icon:Crush](/images/specialties/crush.png) Crush, ![icon:Recycle](/images/specialties/recycle.png) Recycle)

> **Tip:** Place a Storage Box near your Workbench so you can access stored materials directly while [crafting](/crafting).

## Raw Materials

### Stone

The most common material. Pick it up while exploring or use ![icon:Rock Smash](/images/cooking/moves/rock-smash.png) Rock Smash on rocks and boulders. Found in every area.

### Leaf

Pick up while exploring or use ![icon:Cut](/images/cooking/moves/cut.png) Cut on grass and vines. Pokémon like [Venusaur](/pokedex/venusaur) with the ![icon:Litter](/images/specialties/litter.png) Litter specialty will also drop Leaves near their habitat.

### Small Log

The raw material for Lumber. Obtain by:

- Using ![icon:Cut](/images/cooking/moves/cut.png) **Cut** on trees and worn lumber
- Receiving gifts from Pokémon
- Checking sparkling water ripples near beaches

### Sturdy Stick

Pick up while exploring or use ![icon:Cut](/images/cooking/moves/cut.png) Cut on wooden objects. [Cacturne](/pokedex/cacturne) also drops Sturdy Sticks.

### Vine Rope

Use ![icon:Cut](/images/cooking/moves/cut.png) **Cut** on vines, or Pokémon with the ![icon:Litter](/images/specialties/litter.png) Litter specialty like [Bellsprout](/pokedex/bellsprout) and [Weepinbell](/pokedex/weepinbell) will leave Vine Rope near their habitat.

### Squishy Clay

Break clay blocks using ![icon:Rock Smash](/images/cooking/moves/rock-smash.png) **Rock Smash** or **Rollout**. Clay blocks are abundant in **Bleak Beach**. Ground-type Pokémon with the ![icon:Litter](/images/specialties/litter.png) Litter specialty (like [Paldean Wooper](/pokedex/wooper-paldea)) also drop Squishy Clay near their habitat.

### Glowing Mushroom

Found inside caves. Look for them in darker areas of the Withered Wasteland and other cave systems.

### Honey

Inspect sparkling flower beds, or Pokémon with the ![icon:Gather Honey](/images/specialties/gather-honey.png) **Gather Honey** specialty like [Combee](/pokedex/combee) will produce it.

### Meadow Flower

Pick up from flower patches while exploring various areas.

### Fluff

Pokémon with the ![icon:Litter](/images/specialties/litter.png) Litter specialty like [Mareep](/pokedex/mareep) and [Flaaffy](/pokedex/flaaffy) will drop Fluff near their habitat. Essential for crafting soft furniture.

### Twine

Twine is one of the most frequently needed materials. Three ways to get it:

- ![icon:Cut](/images/cooking/moves/cut.png) **Cut textile garbage** — use Cut on cloth piles and cobwebs found in and around abandoned buildings
- ![icon:Litter](/images/specialties/litter.png) **[Spinarak](/pokedex/spinarak) habitat** — Spinarak (Litter specialty) drops Twine near its habitat automatically
- ![icon:Dream Island](/images/specialties/dream-island.png) **Ocean Dream Island** — give a Pikachu Doll to Drifloon to visit. Farm bundles of Twine along with Sea Glass and Seashells (once per day)

> **Tip:** Build [Spinarak](/pokedex/spinarak)'s habitat early for a passive Twine supply.

### Cotton

Similar to Fluff, obtained from Normal/Grass-type Pokémon or found while exploring.

### Thread Scraps

Found while exploring or breaking down fabric-related items.

### Wastepaper

Pick up at **Sparkling Skylands**. This dirty paper is the raw material for clean Paper.

### Nonburnable Garbage

Found at **Bleak Beach**. Can be recycled into Iron Ore by Pokémon with the ![icon:Recycle](/images/specialties/recycle.png) Recycle specialty.

### Sea Glass Fragments

Pick up on Bleak Beach, especially on muk-covered tiles.

### Seashell

Found along the shoreline at **Bleak Beach**.

### Volcanic Ash

Found in volcanic areas. Used to make Glass when processed at a Smelting Furnace.

### Limestone

Abundantly available throughout **Sparkling Skylands** — literally thousands of blocks. The raw material for making Concrete.

### Copper Ore

Break ore blocks in **Rocky Ridges** mines.

### Iron Ore

Break ore blocks in **Rocky Ridges** mines, or give Nonburnable Garbage to a Pokémon with the ![icon:Recycle](/images/specialties/recycle.png) **Recycle** specialty.

### Gold Ore

Break ore blocks in **Rocky Ridges** mines. Rarer than Copper and Iron.

### Pokemetal Fragment

Break ore blocks in the Withered Wasteland and Sparkling Skylands.

### Crystal Fragment

Break large crystals in **Rocky Ridges** mines.

### Smooth Rock

A rare, unique item needed to build the **Smooth Tall Grass Habitat** for [Onix](/pokedex/onix). Only **one** Smooth Rock has been confirmed in the entire game:

1. Go to **Withered Wasteland**, head west from the Pokémon Center
2. Enter the cave on the right, near the water
3. Turn right toward the green building, then right again into a new cave section
4. Continue until you see a punchable rock (for releasing water). Face that rock, then turn around and go left
5. Find the **Poké Ball** containing the Smooth Rock

> **Important:** Since only one exists, save it until you're ready to build the [Onix](/pokedex/onix) habitat.

## How to Get Wheat

Wheat is needed for the Team Initiation Challenge and various [cooking recipes](/cooking). Here's how to get it:

1. **First location:** Rocky Ridgess — find a tattered farmstead with dry wheat that needs watering
2. **Wheat seeds:** Obtained as gifts from Pokémon in Rocky Ridgess, or from red Poké Balls and water ripples
3. **Growing wheat:** Use the **Rototiller** move to set up a field, plant seeds, and water them. Pokémon with the ![icon:Grow](/images/specialties/grow.png) **Grow** specialty will speed up growth
4. **Buying wheat:** Once Rocky Ridgess reaches Environment Level 3, wheat becomes purchasable

> **Note:** You need your team to reach "Great" rank to access Rocky Ridgess.

## Processed Materials

These materials require Pokémon with specific specialties to create.

### How to Get Lumber

![icon:Chop](/images/specialties/chop.png) **Specialty needed:** Chop ([Scyther](/pokedex/scyther), [Pinsir](/pokedex/pinsir), [Heracross](/pokedex/heracross))

Give **Small Logs** to a Pokémon with the Chop specialty. Select **"Look at this"** and choose Small Logs from your inventory.

**Conversion rate:** 10 Small Logs → 50 Lumber

> For a detailed guide, see our [How to Get Lumber](/guides/how-to-get-lumber) guide.

### How to Get Brick

![icon:Burn](/images/specialties/burn.png) **Specialty needed:** Burn ([Torchic](/pokedex/torchic), [Flareon](/pokedex/flareon), [Arcanine](/pokedex/arcanine), [Ninetales](/pokedex/ninetales), [Charmander](/pokedex/charmander))

Give **Squishy Clay** to a Pokémon with the Burn specialty. Select **"Look at this!"** and choose Squishy Clay.

**Conversion rate:** 10 Squishy Clay → 20 Bricks

**Where to find Squishy Clay:**
- Break brown-and-tan striped **clay blocks** at **Bleak Beach** and **Ocean Dream Islands** using ![icon:Rock Smash](/images/cooking/moves/rock-smash.png) Rock Smash
- [Paldean Wooper](/pokedex/wooper-paldea), [Clodsire](/pokedex/clodsire), and [Trapinch](/pokedex/trapinch) (![icon:Litter](/images/specialties/litter.png) Litter specialty) drop clay near their habitat
- Collect from habitat rewards

> **Tip:** Farm Squishy Clay in bulk during each Bleak Beach visit. If you have multiple Burn-specialty Pokémon, assign them all to process clay simultaneously for faster output. Keep a stockpile of 20–40 Bricks before starting construction projects.

### How to Get Paper

![icon:Recycle](/images/specialties/recycle.png) **Specialty needed:** Recycle ([Trubbish](/pokedex/trubbish), [Garbodor](/pokedex/garbodor), [Porygon](/pokedex/porygon), [Cinccino](/pokedex/cinccino))

Give **Wastepaper** to a Pokémon with the Recycle specialty. Wastepaper is found scattered around Sparkling Skylands.

### How to Get Concrete

![icon:Crush](/images/specialties/crush.png) **Specialty needed:** Crush ([Conkeldurr](/pokedex/conkeldurr), [Onix](/pokedex/onix), [Steelix](/pokedex/steelix), [Metagross](/pokedex/metagross), [Tyranitar](/pokedex/tyranitar), [Golem](/pokedex/golem), [Rampardos](/pokedex/rampardos))

Concrete requires a **Concrete Mixer** (crafted with 2 Iron Ingots — see [crafting recipes](/crafting)). Place the mixer near water, add **Limestone**, and have a Pokémon with the Crush specialty process it. The recipe is obtained from [Conkeldurr](/pokedex/conkeldurr) during the Sparkling Skylands story.

**Where to find Limestone:**
- **Sparkling Skylands** — thousands of limestone blocks everywhere
- **Rocky Ridges** — the hot spring cave contains a large deposit

> **Note:** You'll need **50 Concrete** for the Team Initiation Challenge and **25 Concrete** for the Sparkling Skylands Pokémon Center construction. Start stocking up early.

### How to Get Iron Bars

**Requires:** Smelting Furnace + ![icon:Burn](/images/specialties/burn.png) Burn specialty Pokémon

Smelt **Iron Ore** at the Smelting Furnace. A Pokémon with the Burn specialty fires into the furnace to heat it.

### How to Get Copper Bars

**Requires:** Smelting Furnace

Smelt **Copper Ore** at the Smelting Furnace, same process as Iron Bars.

### How to Get Gold Ingots

**Requires:** Smelting Furnace

Smelt **Gold Ore** at the Smelting Furnace. Gold Ore is rarer and found deeper in Rocky Ridges mines.

### How to Get Pokemetal

**Requires:** Smelting Furnace

Smelt **Pokemetal Fragments** at the Smelting Furnace. Used for high-tier items like the Big Storage Box.

### How to Get Glass

**Requires:** Smelting Furnace + ![icon:Burn](/images/specialties/burn.png) Burn specialty

Melt **Volcanic Ash**, **Sand**, or **Sandstone** at the Smelting Furnace.

### How to Get Paint

![icon:Crush](/images/specialties/crush.png) **Specialty needed:** Crush ([Onix](/pokedex/onix), [Conkeldurr](/pokedex/conkeldurr), [Pawmot](/pokedex/pawmot))

Give **Leppa Berries** or other berries to a Pokémon with the Crush specialty. Different berries produce different paint colors.

## Tips for Efficient Material Gathering

1. **Set up ![icon:Litter](/images/specialties/litter.png) Litter Pokémon habitats early** — [Mareep](/pokedex/mareep), [Spinarak](/pokedex/spinarak), and [Bellsprout](/pokedex/bellsprout) passively produce Fluff, Twine, and Vine Rope
2. **Bring max materials each trip** — give 10 raw materials at a time for best efficiency
3. **Check back on processing Pokémon** — you can collect partial batches while they're still working
4. **Explore Dream Islands** — they're dedicated resource hubs with materials, blocks, and recipes
5. **Place Storage Boxes near Workbenches** — access stored materials directly while [crafting](/crafting)
6. **Use ![icon:Gather](/images/specialties/gather.png) Gather Pokémon** — they automatically collect items into Community Boxes, saving you trips
