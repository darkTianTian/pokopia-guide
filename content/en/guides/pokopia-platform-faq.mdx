# Is Pokopia Only on Switch 2? Platform FAQ

Pokémon Pokopia launches on March 5, 2026, and many players are asking: **is Pokopia only on Switch 2?** Can you play it on the original Switch? Is there a Switch 2 Pokopia bundle? This guide answers all your platform questions.

## Is Pokopia Only on Switch 2?

**Yes, Pokémon Pokopia is a Nintendo Switch 2 exclusive.** You cannot purchase or run it natively on the original Nintendo Switch or Switch Lite. It is the first Pokémon game to be exclusive to the Switch 2 platform.

However, original Switch owners can still experience the game in a limited way through **GameShare** — more on that below.

## Is Pokopia on Switch 1?

**Not directly, but there's a workaround.** While you cannot buy or download Pokopia on Switch 1, you can play it through the Switch 2's **GameShare** feature as a guest. Here's how it works:

1. A friend with a Switch 2 owns Pokémon Pokopia
2. Both consoles connect via local wireless (you need to be nearby)
3. Your friend shares the game to your Switch 1 through GameShare

### GameShare Limitations on Switch 1

Playing Pokopia on Switch 1 via GameShare comes with significant restrictions:

- **Local only** — You must be in the same room as the Switch 2 host. Remote sharing is not supported
- **Multiplayer only** — You can only join multiplayer islands. The main story is not accessible
- **No progress saved** — Items collected and Pokémon encountered during the session are not kept
- **2 players max** — GameShare sessions are limited to 2 players at a time
- **Session-based** — The game is no longer playable after the session ends. You'll need a new share each time
- **Membership required** — Both consoles need a Nintendo Switch Online membership and Nintendo Account

### Switch 2 vs Switch 1 (GameShare) Comparison

| Feature | Switch 2 (Full Game) | Switch 1 (GameShare) |
|---------|---------------------|---------------------|
| Main Story | Supported | Not supported |
| Multiplayer | Up to 4 players | Up to 2 players |
| Save Progress | Yes | No |
| Online Play | Supported | Not supported |
| Cloud Island | Supported | Not supported |

If you want the full Pokopia experience, a Switch 2 is required. GameShare is best for trying the game at a friend's house or casual local sessions.

## Pokopia GameShare Explained

GameShare is a system-level feature on Nintendo Switch 2 that lets you temporarily share a game with a nearby console (either Switch 1 or Switch 2).

### How to Use Pokopia GameShare

1. Open Pokémon Pokopia on your Switch 2
2. Select the multiplayer mode in-game
3. Choose "GameShare" to invite a nearby player
4. The other console accepts the invitation and starts playing

### When to Use GameShare

- A friend visits and wants to try Pokopia together
- You haven't decided whether to buy the game and want to try it first
- Casual couch co-op sessions with family

## Cloud Island: Async Multiplayer

Beyond traditional local and online co-op, Pokopia features a unique **Cloud Island** system:

- Create a shared island that multiple players can contribute to
- Other players can join and build even when the island owner is offline
- Up to 4 players can be on the island simultaneously, but the total number of participants is unlimited
- Similar to a Minecraft server — your friends keep building while you're away, and you'll see their progress when you log back in

Cloud Island requires a Nintendo Switch Online membership and is only available on Switch 2.
