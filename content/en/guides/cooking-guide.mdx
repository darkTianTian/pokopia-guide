# Pokopia Cooking Guide: All Recipes, Tools & Effects

Cooking is one of the most useful systems in Pokémon Pokopia. Food fully restores your PP and powers up specific moves, making it essential for exploration and habitat building. This guide covers everything from unlocking cooking to every recipe and its effects.

## How to Unlock Cooking

Cooking is unlocked during the **Rocky Ridges** story (the third island). You'll encounter **Chef Dente**, a special Greedent trapped inside a barrel. After rescuing him, he teaches you how to cook meals that power up your abilities. From that point on, you can cook at any cooking station.

## Cooking Tools

You need to craft 4 cooking tools at a Workbench. Each tool is used for a different type of dish:

| Tool | Materials | Dishes |
|------|-----------|--------|
| Cutting Board | Lumber x2 | Salads |
| Cooking Pot | Copper Bar x2 | Soups |
| Frying Pan | Copper Bar x2 | Hamburger Steaks |
| Bread Oven | Volcanic Ash x4, Stone x2, Small Log x2 | Bread |

**Important:** The Cooking Pot, Frying Pan, and Bread Oven all require a lit fire or cooking stove to function. Place them near a Campfire.

## All Recipes

### Salads (Powers up Leafage)

Salads are the first recipes you learn. They power up your **Leafage** move, letting you grow vines, moss, and plants.

| Recipe | Ingredients | Flavor |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| Simple Salad | 1 Leaf + 1 other ingredient | — |
| Seaweed Salad | 1 Leaf + 1 Seaweed | Bitter |
| Crouton Salad | 1 Leaf + 1 Bread | Spicy |
| Leppa Salad | 1 Leaf + 1 Leppa Berry | Sweet |
| Shredded Salad | 1 Leaf + 1 ingredient + Chop Pokémon | Sour |
| Crushed Berry Salad | 1 Leaf + 1 Chesto Berry + Crush Pokémon | Dry |

### Soups (Powers up Water Gun)

Soups power up your **Water Gun** move, increasing its range and water output.

| Recipe | Ingredients | Flavor |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| Simple Soup | 1 Fresh Water + 2 other ingredients | — |
| Seaweed Soup | 1 Fresh Water + 1 Seaweed + 1 other | Bitter |
| Healthy Soup | 1 Fresh Water + 1 Bean + 1 Leaf | Spicy |
| Mushroom Soup | 1 Fresh Water + 1 Mushroom + 1 other | Dry |

### Hamburger Steaks (Powers up Rock Smash)

Hamburger steaks power up your **Rock Smash** move, letting you break tougher rocks and materials.

| Recipe | Ingredients | Flavor |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| Simple Hamburger Steak | 1 Bean + 3 other ingredients | — |
| Tomato Hamburger Steak | 1 Bean + 1 Tomato + 2 others | Sour |
| Potato Hamburger Steak | 1 Bean + 1 Potato + 2 others | Sweet |
| Mushroom Hamburger Steak | 1 Bean + 1 Mushroom + 2 others | Dry |

### Bread (Powers up Cut)

Bread powers up your **Cut** move, increasing its reach and effectiveness.

| Recipe | Ingredients | Flavor |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| Simple Bread | 1 Wheat + 2 other ingredients | — |
| Carrot Bread | 1 Wheat + 1 Carrot + 1 other | Spicy |
| Leppa Bread | 1 Wheat + 1 Leppa Berry + 1 other | Sour |
| Bread Bowl | 1 Wheat + 1 Soup + 1 other + Burn Pokémon | Spicy |

## Cooking Effects

Eating food provides two benefits:

1. **Fully restores your PP** — the yellow PP gauge is completely refilled
2. **Powers up a specific move** — each dish type enhances a different move:
   - Salads → Leafage (grow plants)
   - Soups → Water Gun (extended range)
   - Hamburger Steaks → Rock Smash (break harder rocks)
   - Bread → Cut (wider reach)

The move buff lasts until you use up the powered-up PP.

## Pokémon Cooking Helpers

Having a Pokémon with a specific specialty nearby while cooking can change the result:

- **Chop specialty** — turns salad into Shredded Salad (e.g., Scyther, Pinsir)
- **Crush specialty** — turns salad into Crushed Berry Salad (e.g., Heracross)
- **Burn specialty** — turns bread into Bread Bowl (e.g., Charmander, Vulpix)

Make sure your helper Pokémon is following you or placed near the cooking station.

## Flavor & Pokémon Preferences

Each dish has a flavor (Sweet, Sour, Spicy, Bitter, Dry). You can gift food to Pokémon matching their flavor preferences to increase their comfort level in habitats. Check each Pokémon's Favorites in the Pokédex to find their preferred flavors.

## Tips

- Always carry ingredients — you never know when you'll need a quick PP refill
- Place cooking stations near your main workbench for convenience
- Craft multiple Campfires — you need fire for 3 out of 4 cooking tools
- Experiment with different Pokémon helpers to discover new recipe variations
