# Pokopia Cooking Guide: All Recipes, Tools & Effects

Cooking is one of the most useful systems in Pokémon Pokopia. Food fully restores your PP and powers up specific moves, making it essential for exploration and [habitat](/habitat) building. This guide covers everything from unlocking cooking to every recipe and its effects. You can also browse all dishes on the [Cooking](/cooking) page.

## How to Unlock Cooking

Cooking is unlocked during the **Rocky Ridges** story (the third island). You'll encounter **Chef Dente**, a special Greedent trapped inside a barrel. After rescuing him, he teaches you how to cook meals that power up your abilities. From that point on, you can cook at any cooking station.

## Cooking Effects

Eating food provides two benefits:

1. **Fully restores your PP** — the yellow PP gauge is completely refilled
2. **Powers up a specific move** — each dish type enhances a different move:
   - ![icon:Grow](/images/specialties/grow.png) Salads → ![icon:Leafage](/images/cooking/moves/leafage.png) Leafage (grow plants)
   - ![icon:Water](/images/specialties/water.png) Soups → ![icon:Water Gun](/images/cooking/moves/water-gun.png) Water Gun (extended range)
   - ![icon:Crush](/images/specialties/crush.png) Hamburger Steaks → ![icon:Rock Smash](/images/cooking/moves/rock-smash.png) Rock Smash (break harder rocks)
   - ![icon:Chop](/images/specialties/chop.png) Bread → ![icon:Cut](/images/cooking/moves/cut.png) Cut (wider reach)

The move buff lasts until you use up the powered-up PP.

## Pokémon Cooking Helpers

Having a Pokémon with a specific specialty nearby while cooking can change the result:

- ![icon:Chop](/images/specialties/chop.png) **Chop specialty** — turns salad into Shredded Salad (e.g., [Scyther](/pokedex/scyther), [Pinsir](/pokedex/pinsir))
- ![icon:Crush](/images/specialties/crush.png) **Crush specialty** — turns salad into Crushed Berry Salad (e.g., [Heracross](/pokedex/heracross))
- ![icon:Burn](/images/specialties/burn.png) **Burn specialty** — turns bread into Bread Bowl (e.g., [Charmander](/pokedex/charmander), [Vulpix](/pokedex/vulpix))

Make sure your helper Pokémon is following you or placed near the cooking station.

## Flavor & Pokémon Preferences

Each dish has a flavor (Sweet, Sour, Spicy, Bitter, Dry). You can gift food to Pokémon matching their flavor preferences to increase their comfort level in [habitats](/habitat). Check each Pokémon's Favorites in the [Pokédex](/pokedex) to find their preferred flavors.

## Tips

- Always carry ingredients — you never know when you'll need a quick PP refill
- Place cooking stations near your main workbench for convenience
- Craft multiple Campfires — you need fire for 3 out of 4 cooking tools
- Experiment with different Pokémon helpers to discover new recipe variations
